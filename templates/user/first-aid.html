First Aid

Bleeding

Bleeding is defined as the loss of blood. The organs and blood vessels contain blood. If any of these is damaged, blood can flow freely inside or outside the body. If blood flows inside the body, it's referred to as internal bleeding.

STEPS:
1. Remove any clothing or debris on the wound
2. Stop the bleeding.
3. Help the injured person lie down
4. Don't remove the gauze or bandage
5. Immobilize the injured body part as much as possible

Burns

A burn is a type of injury to skin, or other tissues, caused by heat, cold, electricity, chemicals, friction, or radiation. Most burns are due to heat from hot liquids, solids, or fire.

STEPS:
1. Protect the burned person from further harm
2. Make certain that the person burned is breathing
3. Remove jewelry, belts and other restrictive items
4. Cover the area of the burn
5. Don't immerse large severe burns in water
6. Elevate the burned area

Shock

Shock is a life-threatening condition that occurs when the body is not getting enough blood flow

STEPS: (Call: 911)
1. Lay the person down and elevate the legs and feet slightly
2. Keep the person still and don't move him or her unless necessary.
3. Begin CPR if the person shows no signs of life, such as not breathing, coughing or moving.
4. Loosen tight clothing and, if needed, cover the person with a blanket to prevent chilling
5. Don't let the person eat or drink anything.
6. If the person vomits or begins bleeding from the mouth, and no spinal injury is suspected, turn him or her onto a side to prevent choking.


Heart Attack

A heart attack generally causes chest pain for more than 15 minutes, but it can also have no symptoms at all. It's important to be aware that symptoms other than chest pain may occur, such as indigestion or persistent neck or jaw pain.

STEPS: (Call: 911)
1. Call 911 or your local emergency number
2. Chew and swallow an aspirin.
3. Take nitroglycerin.
4. Begin CPR if the person is unconscious.
